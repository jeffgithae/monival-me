KoboToolbox vs ODK vs CommCare: choosing your data collection tool

June 2026 · Evidara team

Most NGO teams land on one of three tools for mobile field data collection: KoboToolbox, ODK, or CommCare. All three can build a form and collect a response offline. The differences show up in cost, hosting, and how much technical setup you're willing to take on.

KoboToolbox

Built specifically for humanitarian and development use, KoboToolbox is usually the fastest tool for a non-technical team to get a survey live. Free hosted accounts are available, and the form builder is approachable without training. It's a strong default choice for most NGO M&E teams.

ODK (Open Data Kit)

ODK is the open-source foundation that KoboToolbox itself is built on. Choosing ODK directly usually means self-hosting your own server, which gives more control but requires more technical capacity to set up and maintain. Teams with an in-house developer or strict data residency requirements often prefer this route.

CommCare

CommCare leans further into case management — tracking an individual or household over multiple visits rather than just collecting one-off survey responses. It's the strongest of the three when your fieldwork looks like ongoing casework (health programs, social protection, community health worker visits) rather than periodic surveys.

So which one should you pick?

  • Quick surveys, non-technical team: KoboToolbox.
  • Self-hosting or custom infrastructure needs: ODK.
  • Ongoing case management across visits: CommCare.

The good news: you don't have to choose just for Evidara

Whichever platform fits your fieldwork, Evidara connects to all three — plus Ona — and syncs survey responses directly into your indicators and activities. The decision of which collection tool to use and the decision of how that data reaches your logframe are separate, and they should be.
