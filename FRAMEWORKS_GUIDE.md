# Planning Frameworks Guide - Logframe vs. BSC vs. OKRs

## Overview
Evidara  now supports three complementary strategic planning frameworks. Choose the one that fits your organization, or use them together for comprehensive planning and execution.

---

## Quick Comparison

| Aspect | Logframe | Balanced Scorecard | OKRs |
|--------|----------|-------------------|------|
| **Time Horizon** | 3-5 years (project) | Annual (strategic) | Quarterly (tactical) |
| **Purpose** | Project design & evaluation | Strategy execution | Agile goal achievement |
| **Focus** | Results chain (inputs→outcomes) | 4 strategic perspectives | Ambitious objectives |
| **Update Frequency** | Annual | Monthly | Weekly |
| **Best For** | Donor requirements, evaluation | Strategic alignment | Rapid execution, innovation |
| **Complexity** | Medium | High | Low |
| **Learning Org** | Good for learning | Excellent for strategy | Excellent for execution |

## Framework Details

### Logframe (Already in Evidara )
**What it is:** Logical framework showing assumptions, inputs, activities, outputs, outcomes, and impact

**When to use:**
- ✅ Donor-required planning
- ✅ Long-term project design
- ✅ External evaluation
- ✅ Evidence-based programming

**Example Structure:**
```
Impact (Project goal)
├─ Outcome 1 (Result of activities)
│  ├─ Output 1.1 (Deliverable)
│  │  └─ Activities 1.1.1
│  └─ Output 1.2
│     └─ Activities 1.2.1
└─ Outcome 2
   └─ Outputs & Activities
```

**Timeline:** Project lifecycle (3-5 years typically)

---

### Balanced Scorecard
**What it is:** 4-perspective strategic management system (Financial, Customer, Internal, Learning)

**When to use:**
- ✅ Defining organizational strategy
- ✅ Aligning departments
- ✅ Monitoring strategic health
- ✅ Long-term capability building

**4 Perspectives:**
```
Financial Perspective
  ↓ "How do we create value?"
  Donor revenue, cost control, sustainability

Customer Perspective
  ↓ "How do we serve stakeholders?"
  Beneficiary satisfaction, partner relations, reach

Internal Process Perspective
  ↓ "How do we operate?"
  Efficiency, quality, compliance, innovation

Learning & Growth Perspective
  ↓ "How do we improve?"
  Staff capability, culture, systems
```

**Timeline:** Annual, reviewed monthly

**Example Structure:**
```
2026 Strategic Scorecard
├─ Financial: "Achieve $5M sustainable funding"
├─ Customer: "Reach 500k beneficiaries"
├─ Internal: "95% on-time project delivery"
└─ Learning: "100% staff trained on new systems"
```

---

### OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)
**What it is:** Quarterly goal-setting framework with ambitious stretch targets

**When to use:**
- ✅ Quarterly execution
- ✅ Rapid pivoting
- ✅ Individual/team goals
- ✅ Innovation initiatives

**Structure:**
```
Objective (Inspirational goal)
├─ Key Result 1 (Measurable)
├─ Key Result 2 (Measurable)
└─ Key Result 3 (Measurable)
```

**Timeline:** 13-week quarters, adjusted weekly

**Example Structure:**
```
Q4 2026 OKRs
├─ Objective: "Scale to 3 new countries"
│  ├─ KR1: Launch programs in Rwanda, Uganda, Kenya
│  ├─ KR2: Hire 20 country staff
│  └─ KR3: Reach 200k new beneficiaries
└─ Objective: "Build $10M endowment"
   ├─ KR1: Secure 5 major donor commitments
   ├─ KR2: Establish endowment fund structure
   └─ KR3: Achieve $2M in contributions
```

                          ↓ Executes via

## How to Choose Your Framework(s)

### Use Logframe If:
- ✅ You have donor requirements (most NGOs do)
- ✅ You implement specific projects
- ✅ You need to evaluate impact
- ✅ You follow project management methodology
- ✅ Time horizon: 3-5 years

**Who uses it:** All NGOs (built into Evidara  already)

---

### Add Balanced Scorecard If:
- ✅ You want to manage strategy holistically
- ✅ You need to align multiple departments
- ✅ You have diverse stakeholder interests
- ✅ You focus on long-term sustainability
- ✅ You want to balance financial and non-financial metrics

**Who uses it:** Mature organizations, complex NGOs, federations

**Setup Cost:** Medium (monthly reviews)

---

### Add OKRs If:
- ✅ You need agility and rapid pivoting
- ✅ You want to motivate ambitious goals
- ✅ You have fast-moving team (startup culture)
- ✅ You need to coordinate quarterly sprints
- ✅ You want to empower individual teams

**Who uses it:** Tech startups, growth-stage NGOs, innovation-focused orgs

**Setup Cost:** Low (weekly updates)

## Recommended Combinations

### Scenario 1: Traditional Donor-Focused NGO
```
Use: Logframe ✅
├─ Meet donor requirements
├─ Document project theory of change
└─ Track outputs & outcomes

Optional: Balanced Scorecard (if you have multiple programs)
└─ Align all programs to organizational strategy
```

---

### Scenario 2: Growing Multi-Program NGO
```
Use: Logframe ✅ + Balanced Scorecard ✅
├─ Logframe for each program (donor requirement)
├─ BSC for organizational strategy (annual)
├─ Align programs to organizational objectives
└─ Monitor financial & non-financial health

Optional: OKRs (if rapid growth)
└─ Quarterly sprint planning & execution
```

---

### Scenario 3: Fast-Growing Tech-Enabled NGO
```
Use: All Three ✅✅✅
├─ Logframe for donor accountability
├─ Balanced Scorecard for strategy
├─ OKRs for quarterly execution
├─ Weekly OKR check-ins
├─ Monthly BSC reviews
└─ Annual Logframe updates
```

---

### Scenario 4: Small NGO (under 20 staff)
```
Use: Logframe ✅
└─ Donor requirements

Consider: OKRs instead of BSC (simpler)
├─ Quarterly goal setting
├─ Weekly team check-ins
└─ No complex 4-perspective framework
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Don't...
1. Use all 3 frameworks if you don't need them (complexity overhead)
2. Create OKRs without connecting to strategy (tactical only)
3. Set BSC objectives you can't measure (needs targets)
4. Ignore Logframe because donors require it (compliance risk)
5. Update frameworks infrequently (loses relevance)

### ✅ Do...
1. Start with Logframe (baseline)
2. Add frameworks incrementally
3. Connect frameworks vertically (logframe → BSC → OKRs)
4. Review regularly (BSC monthly, OKRs weekly)
5. Communicate frameworks to team

## Best Practices by Framework

### Logframe
1. **Theory of Change** — Start with impact, work backward
2. **Realistic Assumptions** — Document risks
3. **SMART Indicators** — Specific, measurable targets
4. **Regular Review** — Annual update with stakeholders
5. **Donor Alignment** — Match grant requirements

### Balanced Scorecard
1. **Strategy First** — Define before metrics
2. **Balance** — Don't overload one perspective
3. **Cascading** — Link to department & individual goals
4. **Monthly Review** — Track trends
5. **Adjust Quarterly** — Adapt to circumstances

### OKRs
1. **Ambitious** — Aim for 60-70% confidence
2. **Clear Ownership** — One person per OKR
3. **Frequent Updates** — Weekly progress check-ins
4. **Psychological Safety** — Miss-aligned OKRs okay
5. **Celebrate Wins** — Recognize achievement

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Too Many Frameworks (Overwhelmed)
**Solution:** Start with Logframe, add one framework at a time

### Problem: Frameworks Contradict Each Other
**Solution:** Align vertically - each level supports above

### Problem: OKRs Impossible to Achieve
**Solution:** Lower confidence target, stretch goals should be 60-70%

### Problem: Balanced Scorecard Too Complex
**Solution:** Reduce to 3 objectives per perspective

### Problem: Can't Measure Progress
**Solution:** Make targets quantitative, use meaningful units
