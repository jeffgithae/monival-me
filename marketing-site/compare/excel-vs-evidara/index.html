Honest comparison

Excel got you this far. Here's what actually changes if you move on.

Excel isn't wrong for tracking a logframe — it's free, flexible, and everyone already knows it. But it also breaks down in specific, predictable ways once more than one person needs to touch the same data. Here's where that happens, plainly.

Where the two approaches actually differ
Capability Excel / Google Sheets Evidara
Multiple people editing at once Version conflicts, overwritten cells Built for concurrent use
Field data from KoboToolbox / ODK Manual export & copy-paste Direct sync
Approval before data counts No enforced review step Configurable approval workflow
Who changed what, and when Limited or no history Full audit log

Total flexibility / custom layouts Unlimited Structured by design

Where Excel still wins

We're not going to pretend there's no tradeoff.

  • One-person, one-project tracking — if nobody else needs to touch the data, a spreadsheet is genuinely fine.
  • Totally bespoke analysis — ad-hoc pivot tables and custom calculations are still easier in a spreadsheet.
  • Zero budget situations — Excel is free; Evidara starts at $79/month.

Where it breaks down

The same three failure points, almost every time.

The "which version is current" problem

Once a field officer, an M&E lead, and a finance person all need to touch the same file, someone is working from a stale copy within a week.

No real approval step

A typo or an unverified figure can sit in a results column with nothing stopping it from reaching a donor report.

The report-night scramble

Compiling a donor report becomes a manual, error-prone exercise of copying numbers between three or four files.
